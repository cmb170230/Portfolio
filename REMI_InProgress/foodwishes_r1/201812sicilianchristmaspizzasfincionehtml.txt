<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='https://foodwishes.blogspot.com/2018/12/sicilian-christmas-pizza-sfincione.html'>Sicilian Christmas Pizza (Sfincione) &#8211; Finally, a Pizza with More Carbs</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-wX8A6XPKvW8/XBBXohtgybI/AAAAAAAATak/-ungYLNUxE0B2Z2wXd_hBngc8pj6u2XsACLcBGAs/s1600/IMG_0686.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="336" data-original-width="540" height="199" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-wX8A6XPKvW8/XBBXohtgybI/AAAAAAAATak/-ungYLNUxE0B2Z2wXd_hBngc8pj6u2XsACLcBGAs/s320/IMG_0686.JPG" width="320" /></a></span></span></div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Topping a thick-crust pizza with breadcrumbs might sound
like a strange idea, but it really is the secret sauce behind this Sicilian Christmas
Pizza. Well, that and the actual secret sauce. Speaking of secrets, whether you
tell your friends and family that the sauce is made with onions and anchovies
is your business, but if you think that&#8217;ll turn them off, then maybe keep it to
yourself, at least until they realize it&#8217;s one of the most delicious things
they&#8217;ve ever had. Hey, all&#8217;s fair in love, war, and pizza.
</span></span><br />
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Besides the crispy, cheesy, crumb topping, and super savory
sauce, the other secret to this amazing pizza is the extremely wet and sticky
dough. You could use a more standard pizza dough for this, but it won&#8217;t be the
same experience. One reason we can get away with putting breadcrumbs on the top
is the contrast between that texture, and the feather-light, spongy crust. Besides,
if you use enough oil on your fingers, it&#8217;s really not that hard to work with
anyway.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-WwalAQXb37M/XBBXtqY0xOI/AAAAAAAATao/WiPvuNvIoOcGopNwMzgP-2vTPSup2_BpQCLcBGAs/s1600/IMG_0711.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="372" data-original-width="540" height="220" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-WwalAQXb37M/XBBXtqY0xOI/AAAAAAAATao/WiPvuNvIoOcGopNwMzgP-2vTPSup2_BpQCLcBGAs/s320/IMG_0711.JPG" width="320" /></a></span></span></div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">As usual, I&#8217;m not claiming any kind of authenticity here, so
I don&#8217;t feel bad that I didn&#8217;t use <span style="mso-bidi-font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; mso-fareast-font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;;"><i>casciocavallo</i>,</span> which is the
traditional cheese for this pizza. I ended up using mozzarella, aged provolone,
and pecorino, which I think work very well on this, but having said that, use
whatever melty cheeses you like. The same goes for adding other toppings, but I
can&#8217;t imagine anything making this any more amazing that it already is. Either
way, whether it&#8217;s for Christmas, or another occasion, I really do hope you give
this a try soon. Enjoy!</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><iframe allow="accelerometer; autoplay; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture" allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/kfzi5Jcse98" width="560"></iframe></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Ingredients for a 18" X 13" Sheet Pan:</span></span><br />
<br />
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">For the dough:</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">2 cups warm water (105 to 110 F.)</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 package dry active yeast</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 teaspoon sugar</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">3 tablespoons olive oil</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">4 1/3 cups all-purpose flour, or more as needed to achieve a
very wet, sticky dough</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">For the sauce:</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">2 tablespoons olive oil</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">2 large yellow onions, diced</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 teaspoon red-pepper flakes</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 tablespoon tomato paste</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 teaspoon dried oregano<br />9 oil-packed anchovy fillets</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 1/2 cups marinara or tomato sauce </span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1/4 cup water to rinse sauce container</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">salt to taste</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Note: For best results, sauce should be highly seasoned</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">For the crumb topping:</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">3/4 cup fine plain dry bread crumbs</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 cup finely grated pecorino cheese</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 tablespoon anchovy oil</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">2 tablespoons olive oil</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">For the pizza:</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">3 ounces grated mozzarella</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">3 ounces aged provolone cheese</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">- Bake at 400 F. for about 35 minutes (go as long as you can
without the crumbs on top burning, so that the crust cooks as much as possible)</span></span><br />
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">-- For best results, slide cooked pizza on to a cooling rack to prevent the bottom from getting soggy.&nbsp;</span></span><br />
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">--- If bottom gets soggy, pizza slices can be reheated in a dry pan, which will crisp up the crust nicely.</span></span></div>
