<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='https://foodwishes.blogspot.com/2018/11/lemon-gingersnap-posset-lemon-pudding.html'>Lemon Gingersnap Posset &#8211; A Lemon Pudding to Die For</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-dXaBJj6vmx8/W_3n0sKBQOI/AAAAAAAATZc/k_teD8BTeUwZCM8_sT9pLW8D5WzerlyfACLcBGAs/s1600/IMG_0205.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="561" data-original-width="540" height="320" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-dXaBJj6vmx8/W_3n0sKBQOI/AAAAAAAATZc/k_teD8BTeUwZCM8_sT9pLW8D5WzerlyfACLcBGAs/s320/IMG_0205.JPG" width="308" /></a></span></span></div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">This lemon posset seems too good to be true. Not only does
it feature a rich, thick, luxurious texture, and intense, clean citrus taste,
but it&#8217;s also egg-free, starch-free, and gelatin-free. As if that wasn&#8217;t
enough, it only takes three ingredients, and could not be easier to make.
Notwithstanding its calorie count, this may be the perfect special occasion
dessert.
</span></span><br />
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Like I said in the video, I used lemon since my posset was
being paired with <a href="https://foodwishes.blogspot.com/2015/11/gingersnap-cookies-hot-hot-sweet.html">gingersnap</a> crumbs, but the first time I ever had this it was
done with lime and graham cracker crumbs, and was equally stellar. Next time I
think I&#8217;ll try it with both, since once you experience this magical mixture,
there&#8217;s always going to be a next time. <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp;</span></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">This reminds me a lot of lemon curd, especially with how
perfectly it pairs with fresh seasonal fruit, but the lemon flavor is even more
intense, despite actually using less juice. It&#8217;s also significantly faster and
easier to make. So yes, this does seem too good to be true, and yet somehow it
is, which is why I really do hope you give this lemon posset a try soon. Enjoy!</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><iframe allow="accelerometer; autoplay; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture" allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/_933Sthfx5c" width="560"></iframe></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Makes 4 Portions of Lemon Gingersnap Posset:</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">2 cups heavy cream</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">2/3 cup white sugar</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 generous tablespoon grated lemon zest</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1/4 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1/2 cup crushed <a href="https://foodwishes.blogspot.com/2015/11/gingersnap-cookies-hot-hot-sweet.html">gingersnaps</a>, or other crunchy cookie</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">4 or 5 teaspoons melted butter, or enough to moisten crumbs</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">whipped cream and seasonal fruit to garnish</span></span></div>

Chef John:  Love your videos.  I&#39;d like to give YOU a baking tip!  <br /><br />Instead of pre-baking a pie crust using ceramic balls to keep it from distorting, Put a cast iron fry pan in the oven BEFORE you pre-heat it.  Just be sure it&#39;s upside-down.  <br /><br />Make your pie using uncooked pie dough, then place the prepared pie on top of the upside-down iron fry pan in the oven.  <br /><br />The heat absorbed by the fry pan will assure you a crisp bottom crust.
