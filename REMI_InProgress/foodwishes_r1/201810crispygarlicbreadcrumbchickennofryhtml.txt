<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='https://foodwishes.blogspot.com/2018/10/crispy-garlic-breadcrumb-chicken-no-fry.html'>Crispy Garlic Breadcrumb Chicken &#8211; No Fry, Not Dry, Must Try</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-wSFVJRNjhwA/W9ikYwmeDNI/AAAAAAAATYA/GnBDN6BNyJ0sUYV-IgtcVntMvnNVh97lQCLcBGAs/s1600/IMG_9408.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="382" data-original-width="540" height="226" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-wSFVJRNjhwA/W9ikYwmeDNI/AAAAAAAATYA/GnBDN6BNyJ0sUYV-IgtcVntMvnNVh97lQCLcBGAs/s320/IMG_9408.JPG" width="320" /></a></span></span></div>
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Trying to convert a fried recipe into a baked one is usually
a disappointing exercise in futility, but this crispy garlic breadcrumb chicken
turned out to be a very enjoyable exception.&nbsp;</span></span><br />
<br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">While not exactly the same as a pan-fried,
breaded cutlet, if you want to eat tender, flavorful chicken with lots of
crispy, crunchy bits, then this significantly less-messy method is for you.
</span></span><br />
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">One of the keys to this technique is to use panko
breadcrumbs, which are much larger, and more jagged than regular, fine
breadcrumbs. If you can&#8217;t find them, you can easily make you own. Just pulse
chunks of stale white bread in a food processor until coarsely ground, and then
spread out on a baking sheet, and place in a 275 F. oven until they are dried
and crispy. </span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Above and beyond being easier, this method also allows us to introduce some additional flavor, and moisture, thanks to the &#8220;glue&#8221; we use to
attach the crumbs. I went very simple with my formula, but as I mention in
the video, you can add all sorts of herbs and spices to yours. Either way,
the next time you want crispy, breaded chicken with less mess, and more flavor,
I really do hope you give this a try. Enjoy!</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><iframe allow="accelerometer; autoplay; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture" allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/CG4iYD2L_D0" width="560"></iframe></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Ingredients for 2 portions:</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">2 large boneless, skinless chicken breasts (about 8-10
ounces each)</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">salt, freshly ground black pepper and cayenne to taste</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 tablespoon olive oil for searing</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1/4 cup chicken broth for the pan</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">For the garlic breadcrumbs:</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">3 tablespoons unsalted butter</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">3 finely crushed or minced garlic cloves</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 tablespoon finely chopped Italian parsley</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">2 tablespoons grated Parmigiano-Reggiano</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">For the &#8220;glue:&#8221;</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 tablespoon mayo</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 teaspoon balsamic vinegar</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 1/2 teaspoon Dijon mustard</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">For the optional pan sauce:</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1/3 cup chicken broth</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">juice from 1 lemon</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 teaspoon balsamic vinegar</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste</span></span></div>
