<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='https://foodwishes.blogspot.com/2007/08/finding-new-farmers-market-in-greatest.html'>Finding a New Farmers Market in the Greatest Food City in the World</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<a href="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsITc1zckNI/AAAAAAAAAxI/r30DoEi7VRg/s1600-h/peachsample.jpg"><img id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5098659114336948434" style="margin: 0px 10px 10px 0px; float: left;" alt="" src="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsITc1zckNI/AAAAAAAAAxI/r30DoEi7VRg/s200/peachsample.jpg" border="0"></a><span style=";font-family:trebuchet ms;font-size:85%;"  >Sorry New York, Paris, New Orleans and all other metropolises that would lay claim to that title, but when it comes to food, San Francisco has to be the most blessed place on earth. By the way, when I say &#8220;San Francisco&#8221; I mean the entire Bay Area; that includes our culinary muse (and home of the original <a href="//1.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsITXFzckMI/AAAAAAAAAxA/gz5uUgHD-NE/s1600-h/basil.jpg"><img id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5098659015552700610" style="margin: 0px 0px 10px 10px; float: right;" alt="" src="//1.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsITXFzckMI/AAAAAAAAAxA/gz5uUgHD-NE/s200/basil.jpg" border="0"></a>Gourmet Ghetto) Berkeley, as well as Sonoma and Napa Valleys. Can there be any other place on earth that&#8217;s home to so many great Chefs, amazing restaurants, diverse cultural influences, and magnificent array of local ingredients.<br /><br /><a href="//3.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsITNlzckLI/AAAAAAAAAw4/l8NigXBUz0k/s1600-h/grapes.jpg"><img id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5098658852343943346" style="margin: 0px 10px 10px 0px; float: left;" alt="" src="//3.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsITNlzckLI/AAAAAAAAAw4/l8NigXBUz0k/s200/grapes.jpg" border="0"></a>On a sunny Saturday, in the middle of the day, in the middle of summer, in the middle of the City, I stumbled upon a Farmers Market I didn&#8217;t even know existed. My wife and I were taking a walk to a neighborhood called &#8220;Noe Valley,&#8221; for some picnic supplies. This &#8216;hood <a href="//3.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsIQrlzckBI/AAAAAAAAAvo/8tBvmGE-Nu4/s1600-h/grapes.jpg"></a>is known for it&#8217;s great shopping; a long stretch of small, funky shops selling all things edible and otherwise.<br /><a href="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsITCVzckKI/AAAAAAAAAww/iJyX3Va-hhI/s1600-h/romabin.jpg"><img id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5098658659070415010" style="margin: 0px 0px 10px 10px; float: right;" alt="" src="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsITCVzckKI/AAAAAAAAAww/iJyX3Va-hhI/s200/romabin.jpg" border="0"></a><br /><a href="//3.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsIQelzckAI/AAAAAAAAAvg/xx6DJ1F_8bU/s1600-h/romabin.jpg"></a>About halfway through our expedition I saw a modest collection of tents and booths set up in a small parking lot. As we entered the lot, I realized we were standing in a Farmers Market full of the most colorful and delicious looking fruits and vegetables I had seen all summer.<br /><br /><a href="//3.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsIS2lzckJI/AAAAAAAAAwo/wwqXDvVfNpE/s1600-h/cuc.jpg"><img id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5098658457206952082" style="margin: 0px 10px 10px 0px; float: left;" alt="" src="//3.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsIS2lzckJI/AAAAAAAAAwo/wwqXDvVfNpE/s200/cuc.jpg" border="0"></a>Now, I&#8217;ve been to the large downtown Farmers Markets in San Francisco hundreds of times and, always to my disappointment, have <a href="//3.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsIQQlzcj_I/AAAAAAAAAvY/S2RqlWqnFHg/s1600-h/cuc.jpg"></a>never remembered to bring my camera. So, that I happen to be carrying my camera on this particular walk, and would accidentally find this unknown-to-me market, seemed very ironic. Am I using &#8220;ironic&#8221; correctly here? I hate when people use ironic in the wrong context. Did <a href="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsIQG1zcj-I/AAAAAAAAAvQ/U_-kFWNNKRU/s1600-h/necter.jpg"></a>I just do that? Someone will let me know I&#8217;m sure.<br /><br /><a href="//3.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsIStlzckII/AAAAAAAAAwg/Jtz6V8VyWPA/s1600-h/necter.jpg"><img id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5098658302588129410" style="margin: 0px 0px 10px 10px; float: right;" alt="" src="//3.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsIStlzckII/AAAAAAAAAwg/Jtz6V8VyWPA/s200/necter.jpg" border="0"></a>Anyway, the light was perfect, the tables were piled high, and I snapped away (in between sampling copious amounts of perfectly ripe stone fruit), getting what I think were some pretty good pictures you see throughout this post.<br /><br /><a href="//3.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsISilzckHI/AAAAAAAAAwY/6m7oXzBDgHA/s1600-h/blackplum.jpg"><img id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5098658113609568370" style="margin: 0px 10px 10px 0px; float: left;" alt="" src="//3.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsISilzckHI/AAAAAAAAAwY/6m7oXzBDgHA/s200/blackplum.jpg" border="0"></a>The peaches were perfect. The speckled red and black plums from <a href="//1.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsIPyFzcj9I/AAAAAAAAAvI/4-h2cgfFcbc/s1600-h/blackplum.jpg"></a>Santa Rosa were amazing in both color and taste. The flowering basil screamed at me to make pesto. The seedless Thompson grapes, warmed by the sun called me over for a few samples as the vendor looked on wondering if I was going buy any. <a href="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsIST1zckGI/AAAAAAAAAwQ/AfkpAlFQfyQ/s1600-h/peach.jpg"><img id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5098657860206497890" style="margin: 0px 0px 10px 10px; float: right;" alt="" src="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsIST1zckGI/AAAAAAAAAwQ/AfkpAlFQfyQ/s200/peach.jpg" border="0"></a>Next time, I promise. <a href="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsIPg1zcj8I/AAAAAAAAAvA/zaDGEQ-1l5s/s1600-h/peach.jpg"></a>A serpent&#8217;s nest of heirloom cucumbers sat next to a basket of Roma tomatoes so bright they were practically glowing. I made a mental note to buy some Feta. And, no Farmer&#8217;s Market would be complete without the obligatory toy box of mixed <a href="http://foodwishes.blogspot.com/2007/08/fire-roasting-peppers-beauty-is-way.html">sweet peppers.</a><br /><br /><a href="//3.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsISMlzckFI/AAAAAAAAAwI/Z2yjLBtEu1c/s1600-h/peps.jpg"><img id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5098657735652446290" style="margin: 0px 10px 10px 0px; float: left;" alt="" src="//3.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsISMlzckFI/AAAAAAAAAwI/Z2yjLBtEu1c/s200/peps.jpg" border="0"></a>The only photo here that wasn&#8217;t part of the market is the &#8220;just a scrumptious as it looks&#8221; fig bread. This was from the Noe Valley Bakery a few blocks away. <a href="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsIPVVzcj7I/AAAAAAAAAu4/nKj4p70OAQk/s1600-h/peps.jpg"></a>I&#8217;ve been buying this fig bread for over a decade, always serving it <a href="//3.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsISFlzckEI/AAAAAAAAAwA/5Vp8sFIiQEM/s1600-h/figbread.jpg"><img id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5098657615393361986" style="margin: 0px 0px 10px 10px; float: right;" alt="" src="//3.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsISFlzckEI/AAAAAAAAAwA/5Vp8sFIiQEM/s200/figbread.jpg" border="0"></a>with a ripe Cambazola cheese and fresh strawberries. And with that last shot, I put away the camera, and we headed toward our picnic in Dolores Park. <a href="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/RsIO_Vzcj6I/AAAAAAAAAuw/ftrWmoCIe1s/s1600-h/figbread.jpg"></a>Thank you San Francisco. After all these years you still manage to surprise and seduce me in the most unexpected and wonderful ways.</span>
