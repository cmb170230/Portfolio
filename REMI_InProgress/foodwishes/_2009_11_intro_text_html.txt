<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='https://foodwishes.blogspot.com/2009/11/intro-text.html'>The First Annual Foodbuzz Blogger Festival: The Greatest Food Blogger Event in the History of the World.</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<span style="font-family: verdana;font-size:85%;" ><a onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}" href="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/SvnGzWDTCFI/AAAAAAAAHCI/MvPRua6n1m4/s1600-h/File.jpg"><img style="margin: 0pt 10px 10px 0pt; float: left; cursor: pointer; width: 320px; height: 240px;" src="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/SvnGzWDTCFI/AAAAAAAAHCI/MvPRua6n1m4/File.jpg" alt="" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5402567813402658898" border="0"></a></span><span style="font-family: verdana;font-size:85%;" >I'll say right up front that this is the only food blogger festival I've ever attended, but I can't imagine there has ever been anything to match this weekend's level of fantastic food, extraordinary drink, and amazingly diverse group of attendees.</span><span style="font-family: verdana;font-size:85%;" ><br /></span><p style="font-family: verdana;font-family:Verdana;" ><span style="font-size:85%;">When smart, creative, fun people plan an event, it should come as no surprise that the event ends up being smart, creative, and fun. That was certainly the case this weekend as I attended the <a href="http://www.foodbuzz.com/pages/festival">First Annual Foodbuzz Blogger Festival</a>.<br /><br />It was a smashing success on every level. Super delicious food, thought-provoking seminars and demos, and best of all, a chance to meet and mingle with the fine</span><span style="font-size:85%;">st food bloggers in the country.<br /><br /><a onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}" href="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/SvnIMffBJdI/AAAAAAAAHCQ/r_z4-v03kd0/s1600-h/me.jpg"><img style="margin: 0pt 0pt 10px 10px; float: right; cursor: pointer; width: 320px; height: 240px;" src="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/SvnIMffBJdI/AAAAAAAAHCQ/r_z4-v03kd0/me.jpg" alt="" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5402569344943203794" border="0"></a></span><span style="font-size:85%;">They were all there; from fast-growing newcomers to food blogger royalty like Jaden from <a href="http://steamykitchen.com/">Steamy Kitchen</a>, Pim from <a href="http://chezpim.typepad.com/">Chez Pim</a>, and Elise from <a href="http://simplyrecipes.com/">Simple Recipes</a> (it was Elise's blog that first gave me the notion that blogging professionally could be a reality).<br /><br />It was a glorious weekend of eating food, while talking about food, while talking pictures of food &#8211; for a food blogger, that's the ultimate experience. That, and getting a link on one of the aforementioned blogs.<br /><br />The following recap is woefully incomplete. I made a conscience effort to stay in the moment and enjoy the food and people without being consumed with trying to get every shot and record every bite. Besides, with hundreds of the greatest food bloggers attending, I knew I could just link to their recaps later!<br /><br />What follows is a collection of highlights and pictures from a weekend I will never forget. Thank you <a href="http://www.foodbuzz.com/pages/festival">Foodbuzz</a>!</span></p><p style="font-family: verdana;font-family:Verdana;" class="MsoNormal" ><span style="font-size:85%;">The festivities began on the 8th floor terrace of the <a href="http://www.hotelvitale.com/">Hotel Vitale</a>. The views were incredible, as were the cocktails provided by <a href="http://www.skyy.com/">Skyy Spirits</a>. Bloggers from all over the country greeted each other, sharing similar tales of crazy commenters, blogroll slights, and annoyed spouses. Joie De Vivre chefs Nick Balla and Michelle Mah provided plenty of tasty tidbits, including a spicy bacon-laced mac and cheese that had the crowd buzzing.</span></p><div id="iqrg"  style="text-align: left; font-family: verdana;font-family:verdana;"><p><span style="font-size:85%;"><img style="width: 500px; height: 375px;" src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/blogger_img_proxy/AHs97-nuQeVGl3l-K3acX4aFrg5c7xkbMFMU5z045wbTxQfSQOBYfFtuiu8NWRe-qLiyJCPZRP_Lcn4at3VujGjMxbnQjSMydQO4tcnZeLVKbozcnZ2Q_ZeUCw=s0-d"></span></p></div><p style="font-family: verdana;font-family:Verdana;" ><span style="font-size:85%;">Then we all walked across the Embarcadero to the beautiful Ferry Building for a S.F. Street Food Fare. We were greeted by plates of Chicharrónes (crispy puffed pork skin fries). This one was expertly passed around by Kiersten from Foodbuzz, and one of the festivals driving forces. I've never been to a party where Chicharrónes were passed around that didn't turn out to be a great time, and this was no exception! (photo from Joel at   <a href="http://sixby10.blogspot.com/">Six By 10 Tiny Kitchen</a>)</span></p><div id="rjif"  style="text-align: left; font-family: verdana;font-family:verdana;"><p><span style="font-size:85%;"><img style="width: 500px; height: 426px;" src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/blogger_img_proxy/AHs97-l5jU4NKtBBGh7vtfHoh2B_q0QnOYjO66X3ieMMPuM_mEvJJRyVabH4bQrQ_OEwDk7gfvkQyZGpcTAjsMOgvbph3GnqbyABuNsLQNBzefx95jOyW7nuS9E=s0-d"></span></p></div><p style="font-family: verdana;font-family:Verdana;" class="MsoNormal" ><span style="font-size:85%;">Street food is huge in the City these days and some of our finest practitioners were there, including; Tacolicious, Spencer on the Go, 4505 Meats, and my favorite Roli Roti&#8217;s Porchetta. The porchetta sandwich you see here is simpy one of the most delicious things in the entire city. Succulent pork, crispy skin, caramelized onions, tiny peppercress &#8211; it is a masterpiece of culinary engineering. (Photo from Jo Boston at</span><span style="font-size:85%;"> <a href="http://www.jobostonisafoodie.blogspot.com/">Taking Over the World One Bite at a Time</a>)<br /></span></p><div id="xp13"  style="text-align: left; font-family: verdana;font-family:verdana;"><p><span style="font-size:85%;"><img style="width: 500px; height: 375px;" src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/blogger_img_proxy/AHs97-lLkX2vrqkYv_daB9AAh7X9NYRrcWlCVGcFezMmChv_Bq4BKUgN0blHraoAt_3uKglZVbPNOHwxaUk1-HJv3lpTededaS8RzRmJeiQHUHe3mMoqK6F-7RM=s0-d"></span></p></div><p style="font-family: verdana;font-family:Verdana;" class="MsoNormal" ><span style="font-size:85%;">The next set of photos is from the Saturday Tasting Pavilion at the Metreon's City View. Over 40 participating food producers, wineries, chefs, and bakers, shared their offerings to throngs of camera wielding food bloggers. It was a blast meeting and re-meeting bloggers here from all over &#8211; trading likes and dislikes, business cards, and many laughs.</span></p><div id="voi1"  style="text-align: left; font-family: verdana;font-family:verdana;"><p><span style="font-size:85%;"><img style="width: 500px; height: 375px;" src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/blogger_img_proxy/AHs97-mhVxygp5-lWtyCyIN5iRyyHPp041Y4Boy3WlMyj6TIzmfWGFvA2X5RGKTeH8KRCEw8iPVeMGgyE5VXedVkCGaS8Ulnl3ttz3YDf_xdHfc-il0V17m35w=s0-d"></span></p></div><p style="font-family: verdana;font-family:Verdana;" ><span style="font-size:85%;">This marinated squid and scallop dish from Fuego at the Maya was every bit as delicious as it was beautiful.</span></p><div id="hi9o"  style="text-align: left; font-family: verdana;font-family:Verdana;"><div id="mm84" style="text-align: left;"><p><span style="font-size:85%;"><img style="width: 500px; height: 375px;" src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/blogger_img_proxy/AHs97-ljZo7Yd1VUIfY6rHWLIfbVlumrkQLOkn2mqDx8X-wy3okuBtrfVqtF8j9_bYh1hG1PmvGxXh5oawbGrlH7MaIJ_10mXOrfBruKkJNlYxrb_Xlo_twMtQ=s0-d"></span></p></div><p><span style="font-size:85%;"><a href="http://www.bertolli.us/">Bertolli</a> was a major sponsor of the Foodbuzz Festival, and this simple crostini was one of my favorite bites. Caramelized onions, pinenuts, gorgonzola, and one of their prepared sauces made for a simple, but very satisfying snack. Using prepared sauces as a base for quick appetizers like this is a great trick for holiday entertaining. </span></p><div id="e9ck" style="text-align: left;"><p><span style="font-size:85%;"><img style="width: 500px; height: 375px;" src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/blogger_img_proxy/AHs97-mk0o7dEtPM5L69NMrBOpG6jj0Ry1W4cBHeEXuMzRCTAX5KRpk6aesx2am-WB-ruycGRHziV___-3MZ5pL9O2kir7biilQAP9S4vSYdU6vGDoKyE4xO=s0-d"></span></p></div><p><span style="font-size:85%;">Rouge Beer was sampling something called Morimoto Soba Ale. I didn't know the taciturn Iron Chef was so into beer, but it makes sense, to me there's nothing better with simple, clean Japaneses food than a tall, cold beer. I didn't get an exact translation, but I believe the characters on the bottle read, "It's always Beer-thirty somewhere."</span></p><div id="sxf7" style="text-align: left;"><p><span style="font-size:85%;"><img style="width: 500px; height: 375px;" src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/blogger_img_proxy/AHs97-lWP8ep2z1rLtCHvDBxb-90a4L8CU1HjgjuTPt4YapUz0vqm7Wl942pw_MONzcGXK3izTQxcLvWtzJIjfTK8fPEm5y80Ur9N1Xx5Ul1_2InPe6hWQv57A=s0-d"></span></p></div><p><span style="font-size:85%;">Pranther Ranch Meats were serving these perfectly portioned hot dogs. With all the "fancy" food we had over the course of the weekend, it was great biting into a reminder of what my inner-most foodie always craves. And look at those mustard racing stripes! Perfect.</span></p><div id="klwp" style="text-align: left;"><p><span style="font-size:85%;"><img style="width: 500px; height: 375px;" src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/blogger_img_proxy/AHs97-kx9pWO9wehN6iBwVMmGOj-aMd9nspo5uFLgZ6fY-v-VPHW-mfNErf8bbGTdiliplsIPsOj31sgQDqwZSKFrNZUdvkHUGoPjoXawq_F6AGg0NAZ7xH1ltM=s0-d"></span></p></div><p><span style="font-size:85%;">I didn't have one of these beautiful mini cupcakes, but the way the cloudless blue sky was reflecting off the white tray made me feel happy. Mini cupcakes are huge. </span></p><div id="pz9p" style="text-align: left;"><p><span style="font-size:85%;"><img style="width: 500px; height: 375px;" src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/blogger_img_proxy/AHs97-kC4iTEfGPDv1wyIRX8citN053_YnocGHm6aiq8PZJqz0kOuJtSzUo0kgD1zu2kWv1xbAXxYrVTmW2pLZEHOH_ZpfMS4HFHGUTBAVEQn-oV4tnAbmSG5O4=s0-d"></span></p></div><p><span style="font-size:85%;">I also attended a fun wine tasting seminar by Alder Yarrow, celebrity wine blogger and proprietor of <a href="http://vinography.com/">Vinography.Com</a>. It was called "Underappreciated California Merlots." Alder started the class by saying, "We're here today because of a comment some asshole made in a movie called Sideways." We tasted four great Merlots, and the take-away was it's now safe to buy and serve Merlots again. </span></p><div id="wqlu" style="text-align: left;"><p><span style="font-size:85%;"><img style="width: 500px; height: 375px;" src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/blogger_img_proxy/AHs97-l-1ZY8NeF2UFTmhiVI9u-jFkuNyfHuAKXFgagPFplUnWa7zFRkqvZZvzCbbgMX54xzsXQ8Ybo_YbeFfgy-h-lYdkLfWZ4IonQcQYKd55t_KJzMaxsQiw=s0-d"></span></p></div><p><span style="font-size:85%;">After a 10-mile run and a brief nap (only one of those is true - guess which), it was off to the Foodbuzz Farm-to-Table Dinner &amp; Blog Awards. We enjoyed an incredible dinner by <a href="http://www.outstandinginthefield.com/">Outstanding in the Field</a>, which was served family style on one long table that snaked around and through the <a href="http://www.greenleafsf.com/index.html">Greenleaf Produce Warehouse</a>. The feast was prepared by Chef Dennis Lee of <a href="http://www.namusf.com/">Namu</a> and featured an amazing array of fresh, local, seasonal ingredients.</span></p><p><span style="font-size:85%;">There was a passed amuse bouche of Ahi tonnato on buttery toasts, garnished with gochukaru and parsley. Speaking of gochukaru, for the rest of this post you are going to see lots of strange sounding ingredients listed. Feel free to google them, I'm getting ready to head up to Sonoma for a press trip and just don't have time to research them. All I know is they were GREAT. It was a very memorable meal to say the least.</span></p><div id="gc::" style="text-align: left;"><p><span style="font-size:85%;"><img style="width: 500px; height: 375px;" src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/blogger_img_proxy/AHs97-lJU1RfVKN9k5sRodDEi3Z3HAqumHZrh8n2bptyEvHjpWIUOPJkvk9d5ACKBFdOfTvkJrDbLCXzl8ElGki-ffh81RPvsWWOX0pCoRW9tWdLUTVAdHGNcV8=s0-d"></span></p></div><p><span style="font-size:85%;">There were wooden plates of marinated vegetables on all the tables. The cold, tangy crunch of the quickly pickled vegetables revived our weary tastebuds like a charm. I was now ready for the magic to come.</span></p><div id="h78v" style="text-align: left;"><p><span style="font-size:85%;"><img style="width: 500px; height: 375px;" src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/blogger_img_proxy/AHs97-lFqscgkV76LBICM_7-lpnCk1w9H0QiC4_i7MaFexo6Dy3OEEicR9GEZN2v08es8l5NnQtMmZM0B1f7pDmf5TnkatUnRHDxFC89mF48-B5d7LfMLiMSKT0=s0-d"></span></p></div> <p><span style="font-size:85%;">Next was a super-light mushroom dashi. The clear soup a perfect vehicle for the earthy array of maitake, shimeji, enoki mushrooms.</span></p><div id="ph0m" style="text-align: left;"><p><span style="font-size:85%;"><img style="width: 500px; height: 375px;" src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/blogger_img_proxy/AHs97-kCOaz4O4kYg1SDD_v9Bcak5-GzFDf2eGIVNI5GAID46VpLPBPsOSjQqejY9H0NT1yXrSiRvYdfEFTQoPsWCAdHsUroVYRDgFzPzI6di0ZkjDXxGagIBi4=s0-d"></span></p></div><p><span style="font-size:85%;">Next came an Udon noodle (is that redundant?) topped with grilled Monterey calamari with a browned butter ponzu reduction, cucumber, kaiware, frisée, yellow pear tomatoes, chojang and sesame vinaigrette. It was spicy, but not so much as to cover the staggering layers of flavors these ingredients produced.<br /></span></p><p><span style="font-size:85%;">This was one of the weekend's highlights for me, and brought about a sadness that came from knowing I could never make this myself. The chef had told me earlier that the squid was grilled over imported Japanese white oak. Where the hell am I going to get Japanese white oak?</span></p><div id="bvy9" style="text-align: left;"><p><span style="font-size:85%;"><img style="width: 500px; height: 375px;" src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/blogger_img_proxy/AHs97-mGX-fV59dvLLi2kUPM1L2wXhRyYMREdC54B54E29YzL7uyBmXlRW0SN2o4dRCMIGmcmsNm6rcWEme80HFT-EdMI3CUyyfUrLh3pXb-xgIdjhbs6Lq-ukg=s0-d"></span></p></div><p><span style="font-size:85%;">We enjoyed refreshing seltzer water from these cool blue bottles. It was fantastically funky and a great treat. Next year he needs to sneak in some beer mugs, these wine glasses were just too dainty for the muscular brew.</span></p></div><p><span style="font-size:85%;"><img style="width: 500px; height: 375px;" src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/blogger_img_proxy/AHs97-n7EpucCEdpZUF77oqg_MzlU5PsRwz2rMuFxpSCYO9OLdOS9OldWopjCu27HsmlWZMuHWd9K1EiQu7kNKlNLE_Mxja-e_xToyump3FIA9rABRx2eo8WNEA=s0-d"></span></p><p><span style="font-size:85%;">People kept saying how awesome the salmon was, but it was actually sea trout. I hope I get to see them all again next year!<br /></span></p><p><span style="font-size:85%;">A million thanks to Foodbuzz and <a href="http://www.foodbuzz.com/pages/festival_sponsors/">all the sponsors that made this magical weekend a reality</a>. Thanks to everyone who voted, and stay tuned as I add links to the bottom of this post to other recaps of this wonderful event. Enjoy!</span></p></div>
