<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='https://foodwishes.blogspot.com/2013/11/peruvian-turkey-for-thanksgiving-what.html'>Peruvian Turkey for Thanksgiving &#8211; What? Are You Chicken?</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><a href="//4.bp.blogspot.com/-svfeQO0x9vw/UoRHCWo0keI/AAAAAAAAPcg/IVxOEEMzCX0/s1600/IMG_2707.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="205" src="//4.bp.blogspot.com/-svfeQO0x9vw/UoRHCWo0keI/AAAAAAAAPcg/IVxOEEMzCX0/s320/IMG_2707.JPG" width="320"></a></span></span></div>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">I love, love, love Peruvian-style rotisserie chicken, and
have been meaning to do a video on this magical marinade forever, so it&#8217;s kind
of funny that it makes it Food Wishes debut slathered all over a Thanksgiving
turkey.&nbsp;</span></span><br />
<br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">By the way, to the hundreds of you who requested Peruvian chicken, I
checked with our legal department, and this counts.&nbsp;</span></span><br />
<br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">My usual ethnic food disclaimer applies; I have no idea how
close this is to your &#8220;authentic&#8221; recipe, but based on what I&#8217;ve tasted at some
very good Peruvian restaurants here in San Francisco, I think I did pretty
well. I also think this technique translated beautifully to the much larger
bird.</span></span><br />
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><a href="//3.bp.blogspot.com/-nXoNJPlyJ0U/UoRHGCDVU0I/AAAAAAAAPco/OYvbSl4IOzU/s1600/IMG_2745.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img border="0" height="202" src="//3.bp.blogspot.com/-nXoNJPlyJ0U/UoRHGCDVU0I/AAAAAAAAPco/OYvbSl4IOzU/s320/IMG_2745.JPG" width="320"></a></span></span></div>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Above and beyond the vibrant taste, the spice rub formed an
almost airtight crust during the long, slow roasting, and it was truly one of
the juiciest turkeys I&#8217;ve ever tasted. It was almost reminiscent of some
salt-dough versions I've enjoyed before. </span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">As I mentioned in the video, I took some of the same
ingredients used in a green sauce that&#8217;s usually served along side, and used it
to make a pan gravy. I was very pleased with this last minute experiment, and
it actually reminded me, in taste and texture, of a <a href="http://foodwishes.blogspot.com/2008/07/chicken-chili-or-chile-verde.html"><i>Chile Verde</i></a>, which is never
a bad thing. </span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Below the ingredients, I&#8217;ll give you the rather simple
formula for achieving perfect doneness, which will work no matter how you
flavor your turkey, but if you&#8217;re looking for something deliciously different
on your Thanksgiving table, then I hope you give this Peruvian turkey a try.
Enjoy!</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/clxps2PmceM" width="560"></iframe></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Peruvian Turkey Ingredients: </span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 whole turkey, ready to roast</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">For the spice rub:</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">12 cloves garlic</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 tbsp dried oregano</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">3 tbsp paprika</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 tbsp smoked paprika</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1/2 cup ground cumin</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">2 tbsp freshly ground black pepper</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1/2 cup soy sauce</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1/3 cup vegetable oil</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1/2 cup white vinegar</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Rub turkey all over, and under the breast skin with the rub.
Let sit out at room temp for 1 hour. Rub extra rub inside cavity, but save a
1/4 cup or so to use as a glaze later. Tie legs, season with kosher salt, and
roast at 325 F., for about 15 minutes a pound, or until the internal temp in the
thickest part of the thigh is 170-175 F.&nbsp;
Let rest 20 minutes before carving.</span></span><br />
<br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">*I like to cover the breasts with foil about halfway through the estimated cooking time. I also like to remove it, and brush on any extra spice rub (thinned with a little oil) about 30 minutes before it should be done.&nbsp; </span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">For the sauce:</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 cup crème fraiche</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">juice of one lime</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">I cup chicken broth</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">2 jalapeno</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1/2 cup cilantro</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Place roasting pan (pour off excess fat) on med-high heat.
Puree above and deglaze roasting pan with the mixture. Bring to a boil, and
cook until the mixture thickens into a gravy. Season and serve! </span></span></div>
