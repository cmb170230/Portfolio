<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='https://foodwishes.blogspot.com/2014/03/whole-wheat-ciabatta-not-bad-which-is.html'>Whole Wheat Ciabatta &#8211; Not Bad, Which is Great!</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><a href="//4.bp.blogspot.com/-4w4-nz9LJkI/Uxf_TeFinYI/AAAAAAAAP18/V7DNieWmWqQ/s1600/IMG_5295.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="//4.bp.blogspot.com/-4w4-nz9LJkI/Uxf_TeFinYI/AAAAAAAAP18/V7DNieWmWqQ/s1600/IMG_5295.JPG" height="221" width="320"></a></span></span></div>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">I&#8217;ve never had much of a taste for whole wheat bread, which
is not surprising if you grew up during the Wonder Bread years. Whole wheat
flour is significantly stronger tasting, and its earthy, bitter aftertaste is
the reason white flour is the much preferred choice for, well, everything. </span></span><br />
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Besides the taste, it&#8217;s also a little harder to work with,
and fairly easy to turn out something with a density that would make a brick blush.
But, thanks to many years of requests, I decided to give the old no-knead
ciabatta a higher-fiber makeover. Since I don&#8217;t have much whole wheat baking
experience, I did what any good chef would do&#8230;I didn&#8217;t do any research, and
just tried to figure it out. </span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><a href="//1.bp.blogspot.com/-LwSgu5QFUa0/Uxf__JOhzuI/AAAAAAAAP2E/S6WqL_0ZaR0/s1600/IMG_5311.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img border="0" src="//1.bp.blogspot.com/-LwSgu5QFUa0/Uxf__JOhzuI/AAAAAAAAP2E/S6WqL_0ZaR0/s1600/IMG_5311.JPG" height="247" width="320"></a></span></span></div>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">I was quite happy with the taste and texture, and going
50/50 with the all-purpose flour provided just enough of that crusty, chewy
&#8220;normal&#8221; bread experience, and we still get a decent amount of
whole grain.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">The procedure is straightforward, but as I point out in the
video, pay attention to when you start. I recommend doing the sponge in the
afternoon, mixing the dough at night, and baking it in the morning. Speaking of
which, be sure to dust your dough with flour before covering. I didn&#8217;t, and had
a little sticking problem. </span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">I know many of you have made and enjoyed the <a href="http://foodwishes.blogspot.com/2009/01/no-knead-ciabatta-bread-you-can-believe.html">traditional ciabatta bread we posted</a>, so I&#8217;m looking forward to hearing from those of you
who give this whole wheat version a try. Please let me know, and as always,
enjoy!</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/17FqVbaWn1k" width="560"></iframe></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Ingredients:</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">For the sponge:</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 cup tepid water</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1/4 tsp dry active yeast</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 oz (1/4 cup) rye flour (you can sub wheat flour) </span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">2.25 oz (about 1/2 cup) all-purpose flour</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">2.25 oz (about 1/2 cup) whole wheat flour</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">let sit until very bubbly, about 5 hours</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Then add:</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1/2 cup room temp water</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 3/4 tsp fine salt</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 1/2 tsp honey</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 tbsp polenta</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 tbsp ground flax seed</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">2 tbsp shelled sunflower seeds</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">4.5 oz (about 1 cup) all-purpose flour</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">4.5 oz (about 1 cup) whole wheat flour</span></span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">-Bake at 450 F. for 30-35 minutes </span></span></div>
