<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='https://foodwishes.blogspot.com/2010/12/lamb-caldero-shank-that-wont-break-bank.html'>Lamb Caldero &#8211; A Shank that Won't Break the Bank</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<a href="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TQJp9QqeG6I/AAAAAAAAJcY/np8gNE6mOo4/s1600/lamb.JPG" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}"><img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5549114192039910306" src="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TQJp9QqeG6I/AAAAAAAAJcY/np8gNE6mOo4/s320/lamb.JPG" style="cursor: pointer; float: left; height: 213px; margin: 0pt 10px 10px 0pt; width: 320px;"></a><span style="font-family: verdana; font-size: 85%;">This lovely Latin lamb shanks recipe is the result of a little experiment I just did with the help of my friends from <a href="http://www.imusausa.com/">Imusa</a>. You all know how much I love my <a href="http://www.amazon.com/s/ref=nb_sb_ss_i_1_21?url=search-alias%3Dgarden&amp;field-keywords=le+creuset+dutch+oven&amp;sprefix=le+creuset+dutch+oven">Le Creuset Dutch oven</a>, but I realize the high cost puts it over the budgets of most home cooks. So, I was interested to see how this "caldero," which costs u</span><span style="font-family: verdana; font-size: 85%;">nder $30 would perform.<br /><br />The model they sent me looked like a Dutch oven, but was so incredibly light that I real</span><span style="font-family: verdana; font-size: 85%;">ly wasn't sure how it would work. I assumed the caldero would be fine for the browning step, and it was. My main concern was would the ultra lightweight lid be heavy enough to keep in enough moisture?<br /><br />To my surprise it did very well. As you'll see, we only add a cup of broth to braise the lamb, and it didn't even come close to drying out. So, if you are looking for a really light and inexpensive Dutch oven, this one performed well in regards to cooking t</span><span style="font-family: verdana; font-size: 85%;">he food. Of course, I can't give any opinions on the durability, and how this would stand up to normal wear and tear, but you can check online user reviews for those issues.<br /></span><a href="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TQJsQY9OzfI/AAAAAAAAJco/zqiZuvOdrao/s1600/caldero.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}"><img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5549116719706852850" src="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TQJsQY9OzfI/AAAAAAAAJco/zqiZuvOdrao/s200/caldero.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; float: right; height: 114px; margin: 0pt 0pt 10px 10px; width: 200px;"></a><span style="font-family: verdana; font-size: 85%;">The recipe itself is very straightforward. You don't often see lamb cooked with these spices, and I'm not sure why, as they really work together wonderfully. The final plate ended up being a sort of North African/Central American fusion thing, with the fragrantly spiced meat and peppers resting on a simple bed of couscous.<br /><br />Please note: I was using relatively small lamb shanks for this recipe. If you are using larger hind shanks, then you will need a longer braising time, probably 30 minutes more at least. Either way, I hope you give this comforting lamb recipe a try.
