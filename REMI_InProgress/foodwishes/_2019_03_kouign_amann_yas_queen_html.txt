<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='https://foodwishes.blogspot.com/2019/03/kouign-amann-yas-queen.html'>Kouign-Amann &#8211; Yas Queen! </a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-GrAHVVURRoo/XH8stbiWW0I/AAAAAAAATgs/Fyg_0BSJFYYPsu5_TTgveofyx0H_Ke8LQCLcBGAs/s1600/IMG_3959.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="395" data-original-width="540" height="234" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-GrAHVVURRoo/XH8stbiWW0I/AAAAAAAATgs/Fyg_0BSJFYYPsu5_TTgveofyx0H_Ke8LQCLcBGAs/s320/IMG_3959.JPG" width="320" /></a></span></span></div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">The fact that Kouign-Amann (Pronounced &#8220;Queen-a-mahn&#8221;) have
become a popular item in bakeries across America is quite a tribute to just how
incredible they really are, since to stock something that no one can spell or pronounce
is generally considered a retail sales no-no. As you may know, I pride myself
on mispronouncing things, but even I don&#8217;t like to be corrected by a
salesperson, and their judgmental, I can&#8217;t believe you just said &#8220;kooeegan-aman&#8221;
look. 
</span></span><br />
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Yet, despite the difficult name, they&#8217;ve thrived for the
very simple reason that this is one of the world&#8217;s great pastries. Maybe <i>the</i>
greatest. I guess that depends on who you talk to, but the irresistible
combination of sweet, salty, sticky, buttery, crispy, flaky, and tender, is
hard to beat.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">I guess you could just buy some frozen puff pasty, or
croissant dough, and skip to the last step, but unlike many of those, the base
here is a fairly lean bread dough, which I think is one of the secrets. A
richer milk-based dough, which already contains lots of butter and sugar, won&#8217;t
necessarily provide the same contrast. </span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/--ACeyWgsDxE/XH8sxbjmH-I/AAAAAAAATgw/Z-0PFqD5e-A5HbaH-2sKuSnZYc7tHS9GQCLcBGAs/s1600/IMG_3935.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="348" data-original-width="540" height="206" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/--ACeyWgsDxE/XH8sxbjmH-I/AAAAAAAATgw/Z-0PFqD5e-A5HbaH-2sKuSnZYc7tHS9GQCLcBGAs/s320/IMG_3935.JPG" width="320" /></a></span></span></div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Speaking of secrets, I think the real magic of these is the
salt. Apparently the authentic ones are fairly salty, and just as savory, as
they are sweet, but you don&#8217;t want to over do it. I suggest starting with less
than I call for in your seasoned sugar mix, and then tasting on a wet finger to
see where you&#8217;re at. Then, add more until you think it&#8217;s right. </span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Part of me hopes you have a bakery that does these nearby,
so you can easily taste them for yourself, but another part of me hopes you
don&#8217;t, so you&#8217;ll try to make them. Either way, you&#8217;re in for a huge treat.
Enjoy!</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><iframe allow="accelerometer; autoplay; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture" allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/RK7AAI6Zicw" width="560"></iframe></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Ingredients for 12 Kouign-Amann:</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">For the dough:</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 cup warm water</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 teaspoon dry active yeast</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 tablespoon sugar</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 tablespoon melted unsalted butter</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">2 1/2 cups bread flour, plus more as needed</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 teaspoon kosher salt</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">For the seasoned sugar (mix, taste, and adjust):</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">2/3 cup white sugar</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">2 teaspoons of sea salt or kosher salt (less if you&#8217;re using
a fine table salt)</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">For the rest:</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">8 ounces ice cold unsalted butter (2 sticks) for the pastry</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 tablespoon melted butter for the muffin pan</span></span></div>
