          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry'>
<a name='1501394816552114467'></a>
<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='https://foodwishes.blogspot.com/2012/05/pad-thai-popcorn-how-five-seconds-of.html'>&#8220;Pad Thai&#8221; Popcorn &#8211; How Five Seconds of Late-Night TV Changed the Future of Caramel Corn</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; font-family: Verdana,sans-serif; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-size: small;"><a href="//2.bp.blogspot.com/-zyNjckje5nw/T6f8of3ocpI/AAAAAAAAK90/3gqz7Paw4Sc/IMG_4815.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="289" src="//2.bp.blogspot.com/-zyNjckje5nw/T6f8of3ocpI/AAAAAAAAK90/3gqz7Paw4Sc/IMG_4815.JPG" width="320"></a></span></div>
<div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;">Every once in a great while, I get a food wish from someone
I admire and respect so much that I just can&#8217;t say no. This &#8220;Pad Thai&#8221; popcorn
recipe was their latest request, and as always, I was more than thrilled to
make this very influential person&#8217;s snack dream come true. Who is this mystery
foodie? Me!</span>

</div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;">I was channel surfing late one night, and caught the very tail
end of a show featuring a hot spot in Charleston, SC, called <i><a href="http://theginjoint.com/">The Gin Joint</a>. </i>The
theme was unique bar eats, and the host was talking about something called,
&#8220;Pad Thai&#8221; popcorn. What was that?</span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;">I heard those words, and saw maybe five seconds of footage
of what looked like caramel corn in a basket, and that was it. Frustrated, but
quite excited, I jumped online to find the bar&#8217;s website, and hopefully the
recipe. Nothing.</span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;">There were a few links with people talking about it, and a
couple cell pictures, but no actual official recipe to be found. So, I decided
to give myself a food wish &#8211; figure out how to recreate this
extraordinary-sounding snack. I decided to simply fortify a standard caramel
corn recipe with peanuts, lime, hot chilies, fish sauce, and cilantro. </span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; font-family: Verdana,sans-serif; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-size: small;"><a href="//4.bp.blogspot.com/-gjIO4W6J4Ls/T6f8pNbct8I/AAAAAAAAK98/Rr7qDz10Tqc/IMG_4817.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img border="0" height="240" src="//4.bp.blogspot.com/-gjIO4W6J4Ls/T6f8pNbct8I/AAAAAAAAK98/Rr7qDz10Tqc/IMG_4817.JPG" width="320"></a></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;">Maybe it was beginner&#8217;s luck, or the collective
consciousness of all the poor souls who&#8217;ve tasted that popcorn and desperately
wished they could make it at home, but it came out incredibly well. Crispy,
crunchy, sweet, spicy, aromatic, and awesome! You know I&#8217;m a fan of the
hyperbole, but it&#8217;s with all sincerity that I say, this may be the greatest
caramel popcorn-related snack breakthrough since balls. </span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;">By the way, since this was my first attempt, please feel
free to adjust the ingredients amounts and report back. I think next time I&#8217;ll
add a bit more lime and fish sauce, for a little extra kick. I may also explore using tamarind juice and/or palm sugar. </span><span style="font-size: small;">Anyway, I hope you
give this unbelievably addictive &#8220;Pad Thai&#8221; popcorn a try soon. Enjoy!</span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;"><iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="304" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/yUsWBP2NcUQ?rel=0" width="540"></iframe></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;">Ingredients for about 8 cups of Pad Thai Popcorn:</span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;">1/3 to 1/2 cup popping corn kernels (roughly 8 cups prepared popcorn)</span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;">1 cup finely chopped dry-roasted, salted peanuts</span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;">1/4 cup chopped cilantro</span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;">For the caramel sauce:</span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;">1/2 cup packed brown sugar</span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;">1/4 cup vegetable oil</span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;">1/4 cup corn syrup</span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;">2 or 3 tsp fresh lime juice</span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;">1 tbsp freshly grated lime zest</span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;">1 1/2 tbsp Asian fish sauce (fyi, if you don't use fish sauce, you'll have to add salt)</span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;">1 rounded tablespoon Sambal red chili sauce, or very finely diced fresh hot
chilies</span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;">
<span style="font-size: small;">rounded 1/4 teaspoon baking soda (not baking powder)</span><br />
<span style="font-size: small;">*bake 45 minutes at 250 degrees F., tossing 3 or 4 times during the cooking. </span><br />
