<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='https://foodwishes.blogspot.com/2011/01/my-2010-meal-of-year-brunch-sans-pants.html'>My 2010 "Meal of the Year" &#8211; A Big Easy Brunch "Sans Pants" at Commander&#8217;s Palace</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_tjVIan0I/AAAAAAAAJpc/L9KB1zOExVY/s1600/image9CB.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img src="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_tjVIan0I/AAAAAAAAJpc/L9KB1zOExVY/s320/image9CB.jpg" border="0" height="213" width="320"></a></div><span style="font-size:small;">What a truly amazing food year that was! I worked, played, and ate in 10 different cities, and enjoyed over 200 professionally prepared meals, many created by the country's top chefs. </span><br /><br /><span style="font-size:small;">So, you'd think with so many options, choosing a "Meal of the Year" would be difficult. Well, that's not the case. In fact, it&#8217;s a total no-brainer. And by brain, I actually mean pants.</span><span style="font-size:small;"> </span><br /><br /><span style="font-size:small;">That's right, my meal of the year came as a direct result of not wearing pants. I'd love to explain. This all took place on a press trip Michele and I attended, hosted by the <a href="http://www.neworleanscvb.com/">New Orleans Convention &amp; Visitors Bureau</a>. </span><br /><br /><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TSAFRk4qp0I/AAAAAAAAJp0/JulXxAp80yg/s1600/DPP_xxxx000167.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img src="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TSAFRk4qp0I/AAAAAAAAJp0/JulXxAp80yg/s320/DPP_xxxx000167.JPG" border="0" height="212" width="320"></a></div><span style="font-size:small;">We had been invited down to experience New Orleans' cuisine and seafood post BP oil spill, and since neither of us had ever been before, we jumped at the chance. </span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;">We had already enjoyed several days of incredible food, drinks, and music in the French Quarter, and the trip was to culminate in the Garden District with a special brunch, prepared by three of the city's star chefs, at the venerable <a href="http://www.commanderspalace.com/">Commander&#8217;s Palace</a>. </span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><div style="text-align: left;"><a href="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_YzxI49_I/AAAAAAAAJpQ/9rCKVFBB7JQ/s1600/image0CB.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img src="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_YzxI49_I/AAAAAAAAJpQ/9rCKVFBB7JQ/s1600/image0CB.jpg" border="0"></a></div><span style="font-size:small;">For those of you that don't know, Commander&#8217;s Palace has been considered one of New Orleans' finest restaurants since it was built in 1880. A who's who of great chefs have passed through its bustling kitchen, including Paul Prudhomme and Emeril Lagasse. This day it would be Executive Chef, Tory McPhail (tall hat) teaming up with Donald Link (ball cap) from <a href="http://www.cochonrestaurant.com/">Cochon</a>, and Matt Murphy (right) from <a href="http://www.ritzcarlton.com/en/Properties/NewOrleans/Dining/MBistro/Default.htm">M Bistro at the Ritz Carlton</a>. I was really, really looking forward to this meal.</span><span style="font-size:small;"> </span><br /><br /><span style="font-size:small;">As we waited to be seated, I stared hungrily into the colorfully decorated dining room. The smells were glorious; the scent was some sort of sweet, smoky amalgamation of bacon and butter. I was shaken from my trance as our group was led forward toward the source of these heavenly aromas. Suddenly, I felt a tap on my shoulder.</span><span style="font-size:small;"> </span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_YvkI_NuI/AAAAAAAAJow/Xps_EUTfZGI/s1600/image16CB.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img src="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_YvkI_NuI/AAAAAAAAJow/Xps_EUTfZGI/s1600/image16CB.jpg" border="0"></a></div><span style="font-size:small;">It was co-owner, Ti Adelaide Martin, and she asked if I could step aside and let the others pass. I did, to quizzical looks from my fellow media members. Then I heard something I'll never forget, "I'm sorry," she said with a stern smile, "we can't let you into the dining room without pants." It was true. I was not wearing pants. I was wearing shorts. </span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;">If you've never been to New Orleans in August, let me tell you, it's very humid, and very hot. People say things like, "it may only get up to 95 today." So, since it was brunch, and hot enough outside to accidentally make <i>roux,</i> I decided to go with a nice pair of khaki dress shorts.</span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;">She politely explained to me, and the completely horrified PR rep who had made the plans, that everyone who makes a reservation is told about the dress code, and that in the 130 year history of the establishment, no one has ever been allowed to enter the dining area sans pants. Clearly, my <i>"don't you know who I am?"</i> was not going to work. </span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_ZdBezZpI/AAAAAAAAJpU/xSOu3VBWoBs/s1600/DPP_xxxx000145.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img src="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_ZdBezZpI/AAAAAAAAJpU/xSOu3VBWoBs/s1600/DPP_xxxx000145.JPG" border="0"></a></div><span style="font-size:small;">My heart sank. Talk about a swift kick to the <i>beignets.</i> Michele and I were starving, and crazy with anticipation, and it would have been 30 minutes each way to go back to the hotel. Not only that, all our luggage had already been packed for the return trip to San Francisco following the meal. </span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;">Since everyone was flying out after this event, I had no intention of keeping the other press members waiting. The only option would be to return to the Ritz-Carlton to change, thereby missing out on the first half of the menu.</span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;">Then, just as my exposed knees and I were about to leave in shame, I was introduced to the true meaning of Southern hospitality. Ti told us that while I would not be allowed to enter the dinning room, she had checked with the kitchen, and they were fine with setting up the Chef's table. </span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="//1.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_izwZtsWI/AAAAAAAAJpY/yySRpPLekDw/s1600/cp2.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img src="//1.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_izwZtsWI/AAAAAAAAJpY/yySRpPLekDw/s320/cp2.jpg" border="0" height="240" width="320"></a></div><span style="font-size:small;">I was told this is the hardest reservation in New Orleans, with people calling up to a year in advance. Out of sheer luck, they had decided to not book the table that morning, since the kitchen was hosting the aforementioned visiting chefs.</span><span style="font-size:small;"> </span><br /><br /><span style="font-size:small;">Michele and I looked at each other half-smiling, in total disbelief at our good fortune. A few seconds later we were seated smack in the middle of the most famous kitchen in New Orleans. A few friends from the press group came down to check on our situation, but as soon as they saw where we had landed, their looks of concern quickly turned to envious bemusement. </span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_Yza31RGI/AAAAAAAAJpM/Sf-rrYb7Cw4/s1600/image1CB.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img src="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_Yza31RGI/AAAAAAAAJpM/Sf-rrYb7Cw4/s1600/image1CB.jpg" border="0"></a></div><span style="font-size:small;">What followed was a brunch as magnificent as the setting, and as magical as the strange chain of events that led us to it. I hope you enjoy the photos, and enjoyed the story behind them.</span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;">Executive Chef, Tory McPhail, started things off in grand fashion with an amuse bouche of sweet corn Johnny cakes, pulled pork, soft poached quail egg, Atchafalaya river basin caviar, lemon and champagne gelee, hollandaise, and Louisiana sugarcane. </span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_85-wr9MI/AAAAAAAAJpo/xtXYq_3drec/s1600/hkb.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"></a><a href="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_9P_Cq1xI/AAAAAAAAJps/8-OlEmpV5ew/s1600/image12CB.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img src="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_9P_Cq1xI/AAAAAAAAJps/8-OlEmpV5ew/s1600/image12CB.jpg" border="0"></a><img src="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_85-wr9MI/AAAAAAAAJpo/xtXYq_3drec/s1600/hkb.jpg" border="0"></div><span style="font-size:small;">McPhail is known for his creative interpretations of classic Louisiana cuisine, which he reminded us was "the original fusion cuisine." Chef Tory prides himself on finding the very best local ingredients, and almost 90% of what's used in the restaurant comes from within 100 miles of the back door.</span></div><div  style="font-family:Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div face="Verdana,sans-serif"><span style="font-size:small;">The benefits of such practices were as obvious, as they were delectable with this butter-roasted grouper with chanterelle mushrooms, Louisiana soybeans and crushed local blue crab bisque. </span></div><div face="Verdana,sans-serif"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div face="Verdana,sans-serif"><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_Ywjav6AI/AAAAAAAAJo4/HRq8LwHlm94/s1600/image14CB.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img src="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_Ywjav6AI/AAAAAAAAJo4/HRq8LwHlm94/s1600/image14CB.jpg" border="0"></a></div><span style="font-size:small;">Chef Matt Murphy offered a &#8220;Veux Carre Brunch.&#8221; One gorgeous plate composed of four fabulous dishes. We had already enjoyed several examples of Chef Murphy's culinary skills at M Bistro, in the Ritz Carlton, so I was looking forward to seeing what he would come up with for this special menu. He did not disappoint.</span></div><div face="Verdana,sans-serif"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div face="Verdana,sans-serif"><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_YwPJ43SI/AAAAAAAAJo0/Nw2MKNniGTA/s1600/image15CB.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img src="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_YwPJ43SI/AAAAAAAAJo0/Nw2MKNniGTA/s1600/image15CB.jpg" border="0"></a></div><span style="font-size:small;">By the way, in yet another twist of <i>bonne chance, </i>assisting Chef Murphy was Sous Chef Bradley McGehee (pictured above, far left), a former student of mine at the CCA! It was great seeing him again, and I'm glad he is getting to work with such talented chefs. </span></div><div face="Verdana,sans-serif"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div face="Verdana,sans-serif"><span style="font-size:small;">Alligator "Boudin" Sausage with Stewed Greens</span></div><div face="Verdana,sans-serif"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div face="Verdana,sans-serif"><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_Ysw-CICI/AAAAAAAAJoY/aD5yw2dzW5M/s1600/image6CB.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img src="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_Ysw-CICI/AAAAAAAAJoY/aD5yw2dzW5M/s1600/image6CB.jpg" border="0"></a></div><span style="font-size:small;">Escargot "Croque Madame" with Quail Egg</span></div><div face="Verdana,sans-serif"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_Yt5KVXtI/AAAAAAAAJog/SDUk4W5skhE/s1600/image4CB.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img src="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_Yt5KVXtI/AAAAAAAAJog/SDUk4W5skhE/s1600/image4CB.jpg" border="0"></a></div><span style="font-size:small;">Sweet Potato Biscuit With Truffle Scrambled Egg and Sausage Gravy </span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_YupPyRYI/AAAAAAAAJoo/r9zlBuC6erg/s1600/image2CB.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img src="//2.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_YupPyRYI/AAAAAAAAJoo/r9zlBuC6erg/s1600/image2CB.jpg" border="0"></a></div><span style="font-size:small;">Jumbo Lump Crab Crepe with Goat Cheese, Sauce Choron, and Ghost Pepper Marinated Caviar</span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="//1.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_Ysh7LVoI/AAAAAAAAJoU/aAnXrz1O-NQ/s1600/image7CB.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img src="//1.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_Ysh7LVoI/AAAAAAAAJoU/aAnXrz1O-NQ/s1600/image7CB.jpg" border="0"></a></div><span style="font-size:small;">Donald Link, the James Beard Award-winning Executive Chef at Cochon, offered a hearty, Cajun change of pace to the "haute Creole" we had been enjoying, and presented a Sausage and Oyster Pie with Parmesan Bechamel and local Padrone Peppers. There was nothing light about this dish, except how it made you feel while you ate it.</span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_Yyl49spI/AAAAAAAAJpI/DHHtvJZvIK4/s1600/image10CB.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img src="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_Yyl49spI/AAAAAAAAJpI/DHHtvJZvIK4/s1600/image10CB.jpg" border="0"></a></div><span style="font-size:small;">Not only did we get the all this amazing food, prepared and served by these world-class chefs, but we also were blessed with several signature cocktails, like this "Tequilla Mockingbird."  In addition to being brilliant at finding tables for people without pants, Ti Adelaide Martin is also an accomplished mixologist.</span><span style="font-size:small;"> </span><br /><br /><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TSADTmtmg6I/AAAAAAAAJpw/QwxuxZRN8Rg/s1600/dvs.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img src="//4.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TSADTmtmg6I/AAAAAAAAJpw/QwxuxZRN8Rg/s1600/dvs.jpg" border="0"></a></div><span style="font-size:small;">Speaking of tequila, halfway through brunch, and for no apparent reason, these guys walked into the kitchen and played this to the cooks. Would I be using the local term </span><span style="font-size:small;"><i>"Lagniappe"</i></span><span style="font-size:small;"> correctly to describe this?</span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><br /></div><object height="430" width="540"><param name="movie" value="//www.youtube.com/v/sbtNl9D0VDw?fs=1&amp;hl=en_US"><param name="allowFullScreen" value="true"><param name="allowscriptaccess" value="always"><embed src="//www.youtube.com/v/sbtNl9D0VDw?fs=1&amp;hl=en_US" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" allowscriptaccess="always" allowfullscreen="true" height="430" width="540"></embed></object><br /><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;">As the meal came toward a close, Michele and I discussed how we could tactfully pass on dessert. We knew Commander&#8217;s Palace was known for its decadent desserts, but we were full&#8230;and not regular full, New Orleans' full. We decided we'd just split a plate of something, so as not to insult our hosts. </span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="//3.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_YtXqBM1I/AAAAAAAAJoc/VQ91f8W_5xQ/s1600/image5CB.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img src="//3.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_YtXqBM1I/AAAAAAAAJoc/VQ91f8W_5xQ/s1600/image5CB.jpg" border="0"></a></div><span style="font-size:small;">When we informed the server of our plan, she formed a smile as slow and sweet as molasses before simply saying, "No, that's not going to happen." She returned with a tray containing full orders of five of their most popular desserts, including a classic Crème Brulee, and a you-have-to-try-it-before-you-die Bread Pudding Soufflé. </span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size:small;"><br /></span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="//1.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_YxAEw9hI/AAAAAAAAJo8/PiWEJCQMqk8/s1600/image13CB.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img src="//1.bp.blogspot.com/_TtjHZtf6vtA/TR_YxAEw9hI/AAAAAAAAJo8/PiWEJCQMqk8/s1600/image13CB.jpg" border="0"></a></div><span style="font-size:small;">From the depths of under-dressed despair, to the ecstasy of the ultimate Chef's table, this had been an experience we'd never forget. What a meal, and what a memory. A huge <i>merci beaucoup</i> to <a href="http://www.commanderspalace.com/">Commander&#8217;s Palace</a> for a truly extraordinary brunch, and to Chefs McPhail, Link, Murphy, and McGehee for letting us share their kitchen! </span></div>
