<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='https://foodwishes.blogspot.com/2016/05/steak-pauline-steak-formerly-known-as.html'>Steak Pauline (The Steak Formerly Known as Diane)</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-hQuYA4SAeDM/Vz9QhAiPJhI/AAAAAAAASC0/_v6nvacl-bcDMkHuxwijUp2G17SREBjzgCLcB/s1600/IMG_4883.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="222" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-hQuYA4SAeDM/Vz9QhAiPJhI/AAAAAAAASC0/_v6nvacl-bcDMkHuxwijUp2G17SREBjzgCLcB/s320/IMG_4883.JPG" width="320" /></a></span></span></div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">As you may know, I haven&#8217;t posted for a while due to the sudden
passing of my mother, Pauline. It&#8217;d been a tough few weeks, but she was the
ultimate, <i>&#8220;the show must go on&#8221; </i>kind of lady, and so that&#8217;s what we&#8217;ll do. She
had multiple surgeries in recent years that made it difficult, and often painful,
to move around her kitchen. Despite this, she&#8217;d still somehow manage to bake a
cake (or two), or make a big batch of cookies to bring to whatever family event
she was attending. 
</span></span><br />
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">While cooking and eating with family was her greatest joy,
she also loved going to restaurants. Going out for dinner on Friday night was
one of our great family traditions, and while I don&#8217;t remember having Steak
Diane cooked table-side, this dish represents that bygone era for me. Looking
back, I realize this weekly respite<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"> </span>meant much more to her than just a short break from
cooking and<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"> </span>dishes. </span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-zWPxl1eBKCk/Vz9RmjBYOiI/AAAAAAAASDA/jCntvA2S5IwHMyfH-AM0MM1WtEuiZT8ywCLcB/s1600/IMG_4906.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img border="0" height="240" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-zWPxl1eBKCk/Vz9RmjBYOiI/AAAAAAAASDA/jCntvA2S5IwHMyfH-AM0MM1WtEuiZT8ywCLcB/s320/IMG_4906.JPG" width="320" /></a></span></span></div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><span id="goog_1419941504"></span><span id="goog_1419941505"></span>Before I get into the recipe, I wanted to take a moment to
thank everyone for all the amazing thoughts and prayers I received during the
last couple weeks. I&#8217;ve never met the vast majority of you, but nevertheless,
it felt like I was hearing from hundreds of old friends, who somehow knew
exactly what to say. There&#8217;s no easy way to lose someone you love, but your
kind, comforting words, gave me strength. </span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">With that in mind, I present this incredibly delicious,
Steak Diane, which I&#8217;m hereby renaming Steak Pauline, in my mother&#8217;s honor. Of
course, there&#8217;s no official way to do this, except to simply do it, and hope it
catches on. Even if it doesn&#8217;t, at the very least, many years from now, while
surfing the web, I&#8217;ll stumble across a recipe for it, and I&#8217;ll smile, thinking of her. </span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">The procedure here is very straightforward, and relatively
safe, except maybe for the exploding fireball step. As long as you turn off the
flame, and keep your eyebrows at a safe distance while igniting the liquor,
there shouldn&#8217;t be any real danger, and all those Oo&#8217;s and Ah&#8217;s are well worth
the risk. Hey, that&#8217;s what insurance is for. </span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-icfXeECQTDc/Vz9Rg5wTJ3I/AAAAAAAASC8/PT-XAhOHoIgU9i2Y3bEld2FFLvwdRHCZQCKgB/s1600/Steak%2BDiane.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="193" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-icfXeECQTDc/Vz9Rg5wTJ3I/AAAAAAAASC8/PT-XAhOHoIgU9i2Y3bEld2FFLvwdRHCZQCKgB/s320/Steak%2BDiane.JPG" width="320" /></a></span></span></div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">However, the pyrotechnics are very much for show, and if
you&#8217;re concerned, you don't have to ignite the cognac. The alcohol will still
evaporate as the sauce boils, and the end result will taste the same. By the
way, even if you don&#8217;t ignite the pan with a lighter, it can still flame up
when you turn up the heat to reduce, so you still need to be a little careful. </span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">If you do decide to make this, I hope that above and beyond
calling it <i>&#8220;Steak Pauline,&#8221;</i> you will also enjoy it surrounded by the people you
love. While the flames in the pan eventually die out, the smiles they ignite,
and the memories they produce, will be with you forever. I really hope you give
this a try soon. Enjoy! </span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/IuPGQY0nEkI" width="560"></iframe></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Ingredients for 2 portions:</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">For the sauce mixture:</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 generous tablespoon Dijon mustard</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">2 teaspoons Worcestershire sauce</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 teaspoon tomato paste</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">pinch cayenne pepper</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1/2 cup <a href="http://foodwishes.blogspot.com/2015/03/demi-glace-part-1-feel-veal.html">demi-glace</a> (Or substitute 2 cups rich, low-sodium or
salt-free chicken broth. It will take longer to reduce, but will still produce
a great sauce. Just be careful with the salt.)</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">2 teaspoons vegetable oil</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">two (8-ounce) beef tenderloin steaks, fully trimmed, pounded
to 1/2-inch thick (top sirloin will also work nicely here)</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 tablespoon unsalted butter</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">3 tablespoons finely minced shallots</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1/4 cup Cognac or brandy</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1/4 cup heavy cream </span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">2 teaspoon sliced fresh chives</span></span></div>
