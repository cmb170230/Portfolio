<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='https://foodwishes.blogspot.com/2011/04/throwing-down-with-bobby-flay-at.html'>Throwing Down with Bobby Flay at Hellmann's Club Sandwich</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="//3.bp.blogspot.com/-fWDjWDZp4Uw/Ta_neaf53mI/AAAAAAAAJ9I/SJZtqF4kNhk/s1600/Club-Sandiwich-logo.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="200" src="//3.bp.blogspot.com/-fWDjWDZp4Uw/Ta_neaf53mI/AAAAAAAAJ9I/SJZtqF4kNhk/s200/Club-Sandiwich-logo.jpg" width="149"></a></div><span style="font-size: small;">No, it wasn't actually a throwdown, but this is as close as I'm ever going to get. Here's a little video recap of my <a href="http://www.hellmanns.us/promotions/buildtheperfectsandwich/ClubSandwich.aspx">Hellmann's Club Sandwich</a> experience, where I joined eight other food bloggers for a private sandwich-making demonstration by the one and only, Bobby Flay.</span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br />
</span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Getting advice on how to make a sandwich from a star chef like Bobby Flay may seem like a little bit of overkill; kind of like having Michael Phelps help you with your treading water skills, but as the Iron Chef told us in his intro, making a great sandwich is not as easy as it looks.</span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br />
</span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="//3.bp.blogspot.com/-QuAyqOtF64E/Ta_nsOGv6CI/AAAAAAAAJ9Y/d8Mw0Qd3DdU/s1600/sd.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img border="0" height="240" src="//3.bp.blogspot.com/-QuAyqOtF64E/Ta_nsOGv6CI/AAAAAAAAJ9Y/d8Mw0Qd3DdU/s320/sd.jpg" width="320"></a></div><span style="font-size: small;">In addition to some great adaptations to three classic sandwiches, he also showed off a couple neat twists, like adding mayonnaise to the olive relish for an extra creamy muffaletta, and replacing the traditional sauerkraut in the Rueben with fresh coleslaw. </span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br />
</span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">My favorite trick was something that I'll be demonstrating in an upcoming <a href="http://hellmanns.us/promotions/buildtheperfectsandwich/ClubSandwich.aspx">Club Sandwich</a> post, regarding a very cool way to slice a sandwich. It's basically cutting the sandwich at an angle and on a bias, but it will but much easier to show than explain. </span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br />
</span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="//3.bp.blogspot.com/-w8VtMxqLmyY/Ta_nerUkkfI/AAAAAAAAJ9M/UFS9NU4DVfY/s1600/DPP_Ab000133.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="150" src="//3.bp.blogspot.com/-w8VtMxqLmyY/Ta_nerUkkfI/AAAAAAAAJ9M/UFS9NU4DVfY/s200/DPP_Ab000133.JPG" width="200"></a></div><span style="font-size: small;">Other key sandwich making commandments include spreading your mayo and other condiments on both slices of bread; and being sure to cover the entire slice all the way to the edges. By the way, if you happen have any secret sandwich making tips or tricks of your own, don't be holding out on us. </span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br />
</span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="//2.bp.blogspot.com/-GKD3njN4ge0/Ta_nfRk5WzI/AAAAAAAAJ9U/NyBwrhBBiss/s1600/DPP_Ab000130.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img border="0" height="240" src="//2.bp.blogspot.com/-GKD3njN4ge0/Ta_nfRk5WzI/AAAAAAAAJ9U/NyBwrhBBiss/s320/DPP_Ab000130.JPG" width="320"></a></div><span style="font-size: small;">After the demo, we got to create our own signature sandwich, and as you'll see in the video, I was very happy with how mine came out. I really think the <i>"Mitzeflaywich"</i> could become a bona fide sandwich sensation. Not only is it delicious and easy to make, but the name is <i>super </i>catchy!&nbsp;</span><br />
<br />
<span style="font-size: small;">Stay tuned for upcoming posts dedicated to helping you build your perfect sandwich. Speaking of which, be sure to head over to the <a href="http://hellmanns.us/promotions/buildtheperfectsandwich/Default.aspx">Hellmann's website</a> to enter the <a href="https://secure.hellmanns.us/promotions/buildtheperfectsandwich/Sweeps.aspx">Build Your Perfect Sandwich Sweepstakes</a> where you can win a $5,000 grocery gift card.</span></div><div style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br />
