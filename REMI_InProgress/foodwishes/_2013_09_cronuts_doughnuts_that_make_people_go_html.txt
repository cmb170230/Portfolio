<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='https://foodwishes.blogspot.com/2013/09/cronuts-doughnuts-that-make-people-go.html'>Cronuts! The Doughnuts That Make People Go Nuts! Part 1: The Dough</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><a href="//1.bp.blogspot.com/-_-baiAfm4ok/UilFGG4-anI/AAAAAAAAPHs/VFu7hSiXQrE/IMG_0041.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="208" src="//1.bp.blogspot.com/-_-baiAfm4ok/UilFGG4-anI/AAAAAAAAPHs/VFu7hSiXQrE/IMG_0041.JPG" width="320"></a></span></span></div>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">I&#8217;m assuming that since you&#8217;re on a food blog you've
probably heard about &#8220;cronuts,&#8221; but just in case,
here&#8217;s a quick review.&nbsp;</span></span><br />
<br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">This croissant/doughnut hybrid was invented by Dominique
Ansel at the Dominique Ansel Bakery in New York City. It became an overnight
sensation, and now people stand in line for hours just for a chance at getting
one of the precious few that are made each day.

</span></span><br />
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Why all the hype? Very simple &#8211; it has the shape and flavor of a doughnut, yet features the crispy, flaky texture of a buttery croissant. What&#8217;s not to hype? Anyway, after seeing like two dozen new reports on the
craze, and receiving a scary number of food wishes for it, I decided to give it
a go, if for no other reason than to save a few of my NYC friends the humiliation of being Instagrammed standing in that line.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><a href="//1.bp.blogspot.com/-S1_mTkSbZHM/UilFIk5Mv8I/AAAAAAAAPH0/1nTxCF-6tJ4/IMG_9993.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img border="0" height="213" src="//1.bp.blogspot.com/-S1_mTkSbZHM/UilFIk5Mv8I/AAAAAAAAPH0/1nTxCF-6tJ4/IMG_9993.JPG" width="320"></a></span></span></div>
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Since I&#8217;ve never tasted a cronut, what follows is purely an
educated guess, but I think I got pretty close. Maybe one of you New Yorkers
will mail me one, so I know for sure? My game plan was simple. Make a slightly
sweet, yeasty, doughnut-esque dough, which I&#8217;d then layer with butter, using
the classic croissant technique. </span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">It&#8217;s a procedure I do all the time, as in once, back in
culinary school, thirty years ago. So, instead of going by the book, or even
looking in a book, I winged it, and not only that, I streamlined things too.
Instead painstakingly pounding out perfectly sized slabs of cold butter, I
decided to try simply spreading softened butter instead. I also threw caution
to the wind, and pulled off the rare and terrifying &#8220;double fold and turn,&#8221; and
lived to tell the tale.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Like I said in the video, <a href="http://foodwishes.blogspot.com/2013/09/cronuts-part-2-sights-and-sounds.html">we&#8217;ll cover the final results in Part 2</a>, but spoiler alert&#8230;these were awesome. I did two different versions, one
regular, and one with an extra &#8220;fold and turn&#8221; which resulted in a taller, and
even more impressive cronut. Stay tuned!</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/cIjz-KtpHEQ" width="560"></iframe></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Ingredients for 16 Cronuts:</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 package dry active yeast (2 1/4 tsp)</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1/2 cup warm water (105 degrees F.)</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 teaspoon fine salt</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">2 rounded tablespoons white sugar (add an extra if you want a sweeter 'nut)</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1/2 cup milk</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">2 tablespoons melted butter</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 teaspoon of vanilla extract</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 large egg</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1/8 teaspoon ground nutmeg</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">1 pound all-purpose flour, more as needed</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">6 ounces soft, unsalted, "European-style" butter (12 tablespoons)</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Fist steps:</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">- Combine yeast and warm water, and let sit five minutes.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">- Add the rest of the ingredients, except for the flour and the </span></span><span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">European-style butter</span></span>, and
whisk to combine.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">- Add the flour, and knead for about three minutes or until
a soft sticky dough ball forms.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">- Wrap dough in plastic, and refrigerate for 20 minutes. </span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">- Roll dough out into roughly a 18 x 9-inch rectangle.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal">
<span style="font-family: Verdana,sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">- Proceed with butter as shown!</span></span><br />
